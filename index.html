Unlearning for leaving ignorance

22:18 | April 12, 2026

Most people believe that the more we know, the better we become. It feels like a simple truth. Learn more, understand more, grow more. And honestly, if we look at life closely, it does make sense. Knowledge helps us make better choices, see things clearly, and become a better version of ourselves. But this feels true only as long as we don’t step too far back.
The moment we try to look at things from a much wider angle, something starts to feel incomplete. Not just life in terms of career, relationships, or success but life as a whole. What happens beyond all this? What happens after death? All the thoughts we’ve had, everything we’ve understood. Do they just disappear? Does it all become meaningless once the body stops? And even before life, what was there? Was there anything at all?
We don’t really have answers to these questions, but we also don’t spend much time thinking about them. Maybe because it’s uncomfortable. Or maybe because we’re too busy focusing on what’s in front of us. It makes me wonder if our ignorance is not about lack of knowledge, but about direction. We keep learning, but we don’t always question where that learning is taking us. Is it leading somewhere meaningful? Or are we just moving forward without knowing whether it’s good, bad, or even harmful in the long run?
The strange part is that only humans can do this. We have the ability to think deeply, to question everything, yet we still end up limiting ourselves without realizing it.
Maybe that’s the real paradox of being human.
